Foundation & Footings — Minimal Groundwork

  • Use helical piers or surface-mount post bases on small concrete pads — no deep excavation needed
  • 6×6 PT posts on Simpson ABA66 or equivalent adjustable bases; set pads just below grade
  • UTV + splitter area: compact existing ground, add a thin crusher run layer if soft — no full gravel bed needed
  • Wood bay: stack on 2×4 PT runners laid on grade — keeps wood up, zero groundwork
  • Check frost line depth (36–42" in Catskills) — helical piers go below it without major digging

Airflow for Wood Storage

  • Open front on wood bay is key — never close it in
  • Gaps in back wall siding (1–2" spacing) or hardware cloth panels improve cross-ventilation
  • Stack wood on a raised pallet or 2×4 runners to keep off ground
  • Orient wood bay to prevailing wind if possible

Roof Details

  • Corrugated metal (26 ga.) on 2×6 rafters, 24" OC
  • Ridge cap and closure strips seal against pests / weather
  • Extend eaves 12–18" on front and sides for rain runoff away from wood and UTV
  • Slope roof away from the open front to direct runoff to back

Wolverine X4 Clearance

  • X4 is ~61" wide, ~75" tall (cab) — 10 ft bay gives comfortable clearance
  • Allow at least 18" on each side for doors and access
  • 9 ft front wall height clears the machine with ~12" to spare
  • Consider a wheel stop (4×4 treated) near the back wall
