Build Notes

Foundation & Leveling on Uneven Ground

  1. Lay out 6 post locations with string lines: 4 corners + 1 mid-span on each 16 ft side (8 ft on center).
  2. Dig each hole to frost line — 48 in for Catskills, NY. Adjust for your region: deeper in colder zones, shallower in warmer ones.
  3. Drop 3-4 in of gravel in each hole for drainage. Set a ground-contact-rated PT 6×6 post in each hole, plumb, and pour concrete around it. Brace each post until the concrete sets.
  4. Each post lands at whatever ground elevation it sits at — the variation between holes is absorbed by how much post sticks out above grade.
  5. After concrete cures (24-48 hrs), use a laser level (or a water level if you don't have one) to snap a level line across all 6 post tops.
  6. Cut each post to that line. The result: a perfectly level top-plate plane, even though the ground beneath is not.
  7. Set the double 2×8 PT beams on the cut tops and fasten with structural screws or Simpson post caps.

Roof Details

  • 2×6 rafters at 24 in OC across the 10 ft bay width. Adequate for ~50 psf ground snow load (typical Catskills NY); verify locally if you're in a higher snow zone.
  • 2×4 purlins along the slope. Spacing per the metal-panel manufacturer's spec (usually 24 in).
  • Hurricane ties (Simpson H1 or equivalent) at every rafter-to-beam connection.
  • Shed slope: front low, rear high. Runoff sheds away from the open front. 3/12 pitch keeps the rear wall reasonable (~12 ft) while shedding water and snow.
  • 12 in eave overhang at the front keeps runoff off the threshold.
  • High-side flashing (against the back wall) instead of a ridge cap — shed roofs have no ridge.
  • Foam closure strips at the high and low edges seal corrugations against wind-driven precip and pests.

Defender MAX HD11 Clearance

  • Vehicle dimensions: 162.4 in L × 65 in W × 81.4 in H (13.5 ft × 5.4 ft × 6.8 ft). Wheelbase 117.5 in.
  • Bay width 10 ft (120 in) gives ~27 in total side clearance — enough to open both doors and walk around.
  • Bay depth 16 ft (192 in) gives ~30 in of front+rear clearance combined. Park with the front bumper ~12 in inside the eave drip line; the rear has ~18 in to the back wall.
  • Front wall height 8 ft (96 in) clears the 81.4 in vehicle height by ~15 in — enough for a roof-mounted light bar or accessory rack.
  • A simple PT 2×6 wheel stop bolted to the ground or a beam at ~14 ft from the front prevents the bumper from contacting the back wall.
